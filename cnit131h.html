soup history

Soup types

noodle soup

Noodle soups commonly originate from Asia, and there are variations across East Asia, Southeast Asia, and South Asia.
In China, there are disparities between noodle soups in Chongqing, Lanzhou, Hokkien, and Guangzhou. Each city has their own version of noodle soup. There are also cart noodles in Hong Kong, whcih consist of tea eggs or fish balls. These are usually made by sellers who make noodles out of street carts. North and South Korea and Japan also have buckwheat noodles and ramen noodles, respectively. Ramen is a staple, as Japan introduced ramen to South Korea.

In Southeast Asia, Cambodia has similar noodles to those of China because of Chinese influence. There is also num banchok, which is a breakfast soup. Laotian laksa is a vermicelli soup, but many Laotian noodle dishes consist of white noodles. However, Indonesian noodles are usually yellow and have chicken broth or beef broth in them.

restorative soup

Restorative soups are based on yin and yang in Chinese culture.
If you eat a medicinal and restorative soup, it is oftentimes meant for you to balance your body. There may be too much yin or yang which would throw somebody off balance.

Some common soup ingredients include chicken and chyrsanthemum. There are also fruit soups, such as making soup out of apples and papaya to restore health. Ginger and red ginseng are also common herbal ingredients. These help with digestion and blood circulation.
